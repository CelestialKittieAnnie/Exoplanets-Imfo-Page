Introduction

Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are planets outside our solar system. As of February 2025, there are over 5,800 confirmed exoplanets. These planets are discovered using various methods such as the transit method, radial velocity method, and direct imaging. Exoplanets are diverse, with various sizes, compositions, and atmospheres. Some are gas giants like Jupiter, while others are rocky planets similar to Earth. Studying exoplanets helps us understand planetary formation and the potential for life beyond our solar system.

Exoplanets List

Kepler-186f

Kepler-186f is an Earth-sized exoplanet in the habitable zone of its star. It orbits around the red dwarf star Kepler-186, which is about 500 light-years from Earth in the constellation Cygnus.

Radius: 1.11 Earth radii

Orbital Period: 129.9 days

Discovery Method: Transit Method

Year Discovered: 2014

Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b is an exoplanet orbiting in the habitable zone of the star Proxima Centauri, the closest known star to the Sun, located 4.24 light-years away in the constellation Centaurus.

Mass: 1.17 Earth masses

Orbital Period: 11.2 days

Discovery Method: Radial Velocity Method

Year Discovered: 2016

TRAPPIST-1e

TRAPPIST-1e is an Earth-sized exoplanet in the habitable zone of its star, TRAPPIST-1, located about 39 light-years from Earth in the constellation Aquarius.

Radius: 0.92 Earth radii

Orbital Period: 6.1 days

Discovery Method: Transit Method

Year Discovered: 2017

Kepler-22b

Kepler-22b is an exoplanet in the habitable zone of its star, Kepler-22, located about 600 light-years from Earth. It is one of the first confirmed exoplanets in the habitable zone.

Radius: 2.4 Earth radii

Orbital Period: 290 days

Discovery Method: Transit Method

Year Discovered: 2011

HD 189733b

HD 189733b is a hot Jupiter exoplanet with a deep blue atmosphere due to silicate particles in its clouds. It orbits the star HD 189733, located about 63 light-years from Earth.

Radius: 1.13 Jupiter radii

Orbital Period: 2.2 days

Discovery Method: Transit Method

Year Discovered: 2005

Discovery Methods

Exoplanets are discovered using various methods:

  • Transit Method: Detects the dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it. This method has discovered thousands of exoplanets, including Kepler-186f.
  • Radial Velocity Method: Measures the star's wobble due to the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet. Proxima Centauri b was discovered using this method.
  • Direct Imaging: Captures images of exoplanets by blocking the star's light. This method is challenging but provides direct observations of exoplanets.
  • Gravitational Microlensing: Detects the bending of light from a distant star caused by the gravitational field of an intervening planet.

Missions

Several missions have been launched to discover and study exoplanets:

  • Kepler: Discovered thousands of exoplanets using the transit method. The mission operated from 2009 to 2018.
  • TESS: Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite, launched in 2018, focuses on finding small planets around the brightest stars near Earth.
  • James Webb Space Telescope: Launched in 2021, it will study the atmospheres of exoplanets in greater detail, searching for signs of habitability and potential biosignatures.
  • PLATO: PLAnetary Transits and Oscillations of stars, planned for launch in 2026, will search for Earth-like planets around Sun-like stars.
